8. Key Assumptions, Risks & Confidence

Assumptions

  • Uplift estimates: 5% / 10% / 15% for low / base / high scenarios — assumption-based, no A/B test data in this pass
  • Treatment score: Fixed at 0.4 (no experimental evidence); real experiments would raise confidence significantly
  • Reachability: 60–90% depending on initiative channel; assumes email + in-app targeting
  • CLV: Survival-adjusted using Kaplan-Meier on tenure data, not simple 12×MRR
  • Customer overlap: Each customer assigned to single best initiative (argmax EV) to avoid double-counting

Risks

  • Uplift assumptions are unvalidated — actual lift may be 30-50% lower than base estimates
  • Propensity models trained on observational data; causal uplift modeling requires RCTs
  • Synthetic data calibration may not capture tail behavior of real customer base
  • Customer response rates may vary significantly by segment and channel
  • Execution capacity constraints not modeled — can the team ship all Wave 1 initiatives simultaneously?
  • Market conditions (recession, competition) could alter baseline retention

Confidence Levels

  • High confidence: Customer segmentation, eligibility logic, MRR calculations
  • Medium confidence: Propensity model rankings (AUC-validated), survival CLV estimates
  • Low confidence: Absolute uplift values, revenue projections (require experimental validation)
